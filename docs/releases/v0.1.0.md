Arrival Atlas `v0.1.0` is the first public release of the historical US flight-reliability
explorer.

![Arrival Atlas route explorer](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/heybadrinath/arrival-atlas/v0.1.0/docs/images/arrival-atlas-route.png)

## Highlights

- Compare airlines that actually served a selected route, month, and scheduled departure window
- Review on-time and cancellation rates, median delay, P75, P90, severe-delay rates, and the
  observation count behind every comparison
- Explore monthly trends, delay distributions, departure-time performance, delay causes, route
  volume, carrier coverage, and airport-level reliability
- Search airports by code or city, swap route endpoints, reopen recent routes, and copy shareable
  route links
- Use a responsive, keyboard-friendly interface with explicit loading, no-data, invalid-query,
  small-sample, and network-error states
- Load the public catalog and Parquet partitions through a same-origin `/data` route so browser
  security policies do not block the public GitHub dataset

## Data snapshot

- Official US Bureau of Transportation Statistics records
- Coverage from January 2021 through May 2026
- 65 monthly source partitions and 36.5 million cleaned scheduled-flight rows
- Rankings require at least 100 observed arrivals in a cohort
- Delay percentiles exclude cancelled and diverted flights

## Reliability and transparency

The published metrics are descriptive historical context, not predictions. The repository
includes reproducible pipeline code, exact metric definitions, reconciliation checks, source
provenance, automated tests, monthly refresh automation, and a public aggregate dataset.

## Known boundary

Historical performance cannot predict whether a specific future flight will be delayed or
cancelled. Current schedules, weather, staffing, airport constraints, and airline operations can
produce materially different outcomes.
